ABOUT ME

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a theoretical physicist best known for developing the theory of relativity, which revolutionized modern physics. His famous equation E=mc² laid the foundation for nuclear energy. In 1921, he received the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the photoelectric effect, which contributed to quantum mechanics. Later, he emigrated to the U.S. and worked at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton. Beyond science, he was a strong advocate for peace and civil rights, leaving a lasting impact on both science and society.
